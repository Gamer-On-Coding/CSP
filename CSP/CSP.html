How Tourism has Impacted the Lake

Findings on Kodaikanal Tourism:

  • Tourist Satisfaction Factors: The climate, local hospitality, and information available before arrival strongly correlate with tourist satisfaction. Meaning that most tourists are driven to take a vacation in kodaikanal due to these factors.

  • Demographic Differences: Large differences between satisfaction levels of different genders, nationalities, and ages. It shows that 36-45 are the most satisfied age demographic among kodaikanal tourists

  • Environmental Awareness: Younger and more educated tourists are more involved in environmental conservation. While there is a clear difference in how men and women view conservation efforts.

  • Growth of Tourism: Domestic tourist numbers grew from about 1.4 million in 1993 to nearly 4.8 million in 2009. Foreign tourist numbers also increased from 1993 to 2009, foreign tourist arrivals in Kodaikanal increased from 37,110 in 1993 to 142,946 in 2009. This represents an increase of 105,836 foreign tourists over that period..

Impacts on the Kodaikanal Lake:

  • Environmental Degradation: The rise in tourist numbers has significantly increased pollution levels in Kodaikanal Lake. This is primarily due to inadequate hotel drainage systems and poor waste management practices, leading to runoff that contaminates the lake.

  • Impact on Local Communities: The influx of tourists has affected local communities by increasing the cost of living and straining resources. Residents may find it harder to access basic services and amenities, leading to tension between tourists and locals as they compete for limited resources.

  • Loss of Biodiversity: Increased tourism has led to habitat destruction and a decline in local wildlife populations. As natural areas are developed for tourist facilities, many species face threats to their habitats, resulting in reduced biodiversity and disruptions to the local ecosystem.

  • Water Resource Depletion: The rising number of visitors has intensified the demand for water resources, leading to over-extraction from the Lake. This depletion not only affects the availability of water for residents but also impacts the lake's ecosystem, threatening aquatic life and vegetation.
